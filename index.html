Software Engineering Intern @ Alarm.com

This past summer, I worked at Alarm.com, where I worked on a full-stack project involving Ember.js, C#, Microsoft SQL Server, and ASP.NET

While I cannot disclose specific details of the project openly on this public site, I want to highlight some skills and technologies I used. I created pages in both Ember.js and ASP.NET, interacted heavily with SQL databases, and used C# to execute SQL procedures I created and interact with OpenAI APIs.

Through this internship, I really got a taste of what it's like to work as a software engineer, getting experience with the non-technical parts as well, including using JIRA, daily stand-ups, communicating with PMs and QEs regarding tickets, and the work environment in general.


UX Designer @ Develop for Good

Develop for Good is an organization that matches students around the country with various nonprofits to do technical work.

On the side in Summer 2023, I worked as a designer for Lit Path, Inc., a non-profit dedicated to helping individuals struggling with addiction and seeking therapy, but who may not have the resources to seek adequate help.

While I am not pursuing UX design at this time, this part-time role allowed me to see what goes into creating designs and participate in each step of the design process, from ideation and storyboarding to creating hi-fidelity designs and handing off to engineers; this has provided me with valuable insights when working with frontend code and specific important design concepts.


Programming Intern @ KORE Wireless

In summer 2022, I worked remotely for the Atlanta-based company KORE Wireless, where I got to become familiar with IoT and embedded systems.

My role with KORE was to create automated test scripts to monitor IoT devices' functionality. I did this primarily in micropython, but for many of these tests, I had to create an endpoint in Node-RED to ping or receive a response from.

Through this internship, I got a feel for working with embedded systems: in my case, a BG-95 modem which was connected to an ESP-32 microcontroller. I learned a lot about IoT, and being my first tech internship, I got familiar with many industry tools, both technical and non-technical.
